Key Milestones in the Development of Computers

The 1946 Eniac

The Electronic Numerical Integrator and Computer was developed by
John Presper Eckert and John W. Mauchly at the University of Pennsylvania.
According to CNBC.

IBM's Personal Computer 1981

IBM released the personal computer in August of 1981. Others had been
available before it but IBM's laid a foundation for future PC's. It costed
$1,565 and had 40K of read only memory and 16K of user memory. It also came
with a built in speaker and it could run self-diagnostic checks.

Apple’s Macintosh 1984

Steve Jobs released the first Macintosh in 1984. It had the ability to
talk and at the time had an advanced drawing application as well.
The Macintosh was designed for 2 main markets, the knowledge workers and the
college workers.

Micrososft Launches Windows 1985

Windows 1.o was releaseed in November 1985 as an operating system with a
graphical interface. Millions of people still use Windows today.

The World Wide Web 1989

In 1989, a British computer scientist by the name of Tim Berners-Lee had
thought of a “distributed information system” at CERN, which is the European
Organization for Nuclear Research, located in Geneva, Switzerland. One year
after, the fist website and server went live at CERN.
